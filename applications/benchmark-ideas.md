# Benchmark Dataset Ideas

This document lists ideas for benchmark datasets that may be used to evaluate E2R implementations.

This document is informational and is not part of the E2R Core Specification.

## Purpose

Benchmark datasets provide common examples for testing:

- Validation
- Import and export
- Visualization
- Performance
- Interoperability
- User interface
- Application behavior

The benchmark suite should evolve as the E2R ecosystem grows.

## Minimal Datasets

Very small datasets intended for basic validation.

Examples include:

- Empty dataset
- Single entity
- Single event
- Two entities with one relation
- Entity with multiple aliases

## Timeline Benchmarks

Datasets focused on chronological information.

Examples include:

- Historical timeline
- Biography
- Software release history
- Medical history
- Investigation timeline
- Fictional chronology

## Relationship Benchmarks

Datasets focused on graph structure.

Examples include:

- Social network
- Organization chart
- Character relationships
- Dependency graph
- Knowledge graph

## Family Benchmarks

Datasets focused on genealogy.

Examples include:

- Nuclear family
- Extended family
- Royal family
- Fictional genealogy
- Large collaborative family tree

## Investigation Benchmarks

Datasets representing investigations.

Examples include:

- Detective case
- Missing person
- Cybersecurity incident
- Historical mystery
- Intelligence analysis

## Worldbuilding Benchmarks

Datasets representing fictional worlds.

Examples include:

- Fantasy kingdom
- Science fiction civilization
- Alternate history
- Role-playing campaign

## Knowledge Benchmarks

Datasets focused on structured knowledge.

Examples include:

- Encyclopedia
- Technical documentation
- Research knowledge
- Historical archive

## Citation Benchmarks

Datasets representing scholarly or legal references.

Examples include:

- Citation network
- Patent references
- Legal precedents
- Technical standards

## OSINT Benchmarks

Datasets representing publicly available information.

Examples include:

- Organization network
- Company ownership
- Public infrastructure
- Historical organizations

## Large-scale Benchmarks

Datasets intended for performance evaluation.

Examples include:

- Hundreds of objects
- Thousands of objects
- Hundreds of thousands of objects
- Millions of objects

Applications may use these datasets to evaluate rendering, searching, navigation, and memory usage.

## AI-generated Benchmarks

Benchmark datasets may be generated using Scenario Generator and Character Generator applications.

Generated datasets should be reviewed before inclusion in an official benchmark collection.

## Design Principles

Benchmark datasets should:

- Be reusable
- Be well documented
- Be deterministic when possible
- Cover diverse application domains
- Encourage interoperability across implementations
